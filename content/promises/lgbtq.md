Mamdani committed to making NYC an LGBTQ+ sanctuary city — a direct response to the federal hostility under Trump's second term. He has framed this not as abstract tolerance but as material protection: funding, services, legal defense, and institutional power for LGBTQIA+ New Yorkers at a time when the federal government is actively hostile.

## Office of LGBTQIA+ Affairs

On March 13, 2026, Mamdani signed an executive order establishing the Mayor's Office of LGBTQIA+ Affairs and appointed Taylor Brown as its director. Brown, previously an Assistant Attorney General in the Civil Rights Bureau of the New York State AG's office, became the first transgender person to lead a New York City office or agency and the highest-ranking trans person in the history of city government. She spent nearly seven years at Lambda Legal and the ACLU's LGBTQ+ & HIV Project before that, leading constitutional litigation on transgender healthcare, education, and employment rights.

The office absorbs and expands the NYC Unity Project, which launched in 2017 as the city's first coordinated LGBTQIA+ initiative. Its mandate includes overseeing LGBTQIA+ programming across city agencies, developing legal resources to uphold sanctuary protections, advancing anti-discrimination measures, and providing support for LGBTQIA+ people and families fleeing persecution in other states.

The campaign had proposed $87 million in funding for the office covering housing, mental health services, trans-led organizations, workforce development, healthcare, education, and legal support. Whether that full amount materializes in the executive budget remains to be seen — the preliminary budget didn't include it, and like most of Mamdani's transformative proposals, it depends on revenue from Albany.

## Schools

LGBTQIA+ educational liaisons in every school district. Currently there are only two liaisons for 1,800 schools — the proposal allocates $3 million to expand this dramatically.

The plan would also codify Transgender and Gender Expansive Guidelines across DOE, making protections institutional rather than discretionary. This matters because discretionary protections can be reversed by a future administration or ignored by individual principals. Codification means schools can't simply opt out.

## Healthcare

Committed to coordinating with the AG and DAs to investigate hospitals denying gender-affirming care. The $65 million investment in H+H for gender-affirming care (see [[promises/healthcare|healthcare]]) is the concrete budget action here — city money backfilling gender-affirming care that's losing Medicaid coverage under Trump.

## The Federal Context

This is all happening against a backdrop of escalating federal attacks on LGBTQIA+ rights. The Trump administration has been rolling back protections, and the appointment of a trans woman to lead a city office is a deliberate counter-signal. Deputy Mayor Julie Su framed it through economic justice: housing insecurity, workplace discrimination, and healthcare access problems "are felt disproportionately" by LGBTQ+ New Yorkers. The office is meant to be a coordinating body that treats these as interconnected material problems, not just identity issues.
