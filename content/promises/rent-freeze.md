Four-year freeze on rents for all ~1 million rent-stabilized apartments, affecting roughly 2 million tenants.

Why? RGB data shows landlord profits rising 12% annually while wages stagnated.

## What

The mayor appoints all nine members of the Rent Guidelines Board, which votes annually on how much landlords can raise rents on stabilized apartments. Mamdani promised to **appoint members who would vote for a freeze.**

Buildings with the worst code violations would receive zero increases even if other buildings get small ones. Landlords who don't maintain their buildings don't get to raise rents.

Some landlords, particularly those with older buildings or smaller portfolios, genuinely can't cover costs. The RGB has a hardship exemption process where landlords can prove financial distress and get permission to raise rents anyway. Mamdani said he'd preserve this for legitimate cases while cracking down on abuse.

### Landlords will "exit the market"

Liberals argue a rent freeze will cause landlords to defer maintenance or exit the market. Think about what this claim is actually saying. The "exit the market" threat often just means selling the property to someone else, not demolishing it. A landlord "exits" by selling the building but the building is still there. The new owner might actually be fine with the frozen rents because they bought in at a price that already accounts for them.

The honest framing would be "if you limit how much owners can extract from tenants, owners will use every lever they have, including letting buildings rot, to reassert their extractive power", which, fine, that's probably true as a description of how property owners behave, but Mamdani also has direct control over regulation of exactly that.

Regardless, Mamdani acknowledged this and said he'd push the state to lower property taxes and insurance costs for landlords, offsetting the freeze. This requires action from Albany, not just City Hall.
