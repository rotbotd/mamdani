Mamdani's climate platform centers on building decarbonization, renewable energy expansion, and environmental justice — with the Green Schools plan as the most concrete commitment.

## All-Electric Buildings Act

Mamdani backed the legislation banning fossil-fuel equipment (including gas stoves) in new buildings. He supported extending J-51 tax incentives for Local Law 97 compliance — helping existing buildings meet the emissions reduction mandates.

## Build Public Renewables

As an assemblyman, Mamdani pushed the Build Public Renewables Act, which requires NYPA to develop renewable energy projects. The goal is public ownership of new renewable capacity rather than relying on private utilities.

## Peaker Plants

He organized against gas-fired peaker plant expansion in Astoria, near his Assembly district. These plants — used during peak demand — are some of the dirtiest power sources and disproportionately affect environmental justice communities.

Advocated for shutting down fossil fuel plants near schools.

## Green Schools

The most concrete climate commitment: a $3.3 billion plan to retrofit 500 school buildings with rooftop solar and upgraded HVAC, build 500 green schoolyards (replacing heat-absorbing asphalt), convert 50 schools into year-round resilience hubs, create 15,000 union jobs, and save ~$275 million/year in energy costs.

See [[promises/green-schools|Green Schools]] for details.

## Resilience

Climate resilience planning for floods, extreme heat, and storms. The resilience hubs in schools are part of this — giving neighborhoods cooling centers during heat waves and emergency shelter during storms.

## Status

The Green Schools plan has been announced but funding hasn't appeared in the preliminary budget. Like most of Mamdani's transformative proposals, it awaits the Albany tax revenue that hasn't materialized.
