Mamdani supports single-payer healthcare at the state level (the New York Health Act) but as mayor his direct power is limited to NYC Health + Hospitals, the largest public health system in the country, and city-funded health programs. His approach has been to use city resources to fill gaps created by federal hostility under Trump — treating H+H as a bulwark against federal healthcare rollbacks.

He appointed Helen Arteaga Landaverde as Deputy Mayor for Health and Human Services. She previously ran H+H Elmhurst, the Queens hospital that became internationally known during COVID's first wave.

## Gender-Affirming Care

A $65 million investment in public healthcare providers to cover gender-affirming care losing Medicaid coverage under Trump. This is city money going to H+H to fill gaps created by federal hostility.

## Reproductive Health

Pledged to double city funding for the New York Abortion Access Fund and the Abortion Access Hub. Launched two new comprehensive healthcare clinics for ages 16-25 at NYC Health + Hospitals.

## Maternal and Early Childhood

$20 million investment in Strong Foundations — a perinatal and early childhood mental health program addressing postpartum depression and early intervention.

## Leadership

Renominated Dr. Mitchell Katz to lead H+H — continuity from the Adams administration on healthcare operations.

## The State-Level Fight

The real action on healthcare is in Albany. The New York Health Act would create single-payer for the state, eliminating private insurance. Senator Gustavo Rivera is once again backing it in 2026, framing it as a response to federal Medicaid threats. Mamdani supports it but can't pass it from City Hall. His role is advocacy and using the bully pulpit — and demonstrating through H+H what public healthcare provision can look like.

## NYC Health + Hospitals

H+H is often invisible to New Yorkers with private insurance, but it's the safety net that catches everyone else: the uninsured, undocumented immigrants, Medicaid recipients, the incarcerated, and anyone who shows up at a public hospital. Eleven acute care hospitals, five post-acute facilities, dozens of community health centers. Over a million patient visits per year. It loses money by design — that's what public provision means.

Under Trump's second term, H+H faces potential Medicaid cuts, threats to coverage for undocumented patients, and attacks on gender-affirming and reproductive care. Mamdani's strategy has been to use city funds to backfill federal cuts, essentially treating H+H as municipal insurance for the most vulnerable.
