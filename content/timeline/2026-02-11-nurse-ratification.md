---
title: "Nurses at Montefiore and Mount Sinai Ratify New Contracts"
date: 2026-02-11
tags: [labor, healthcare]
---

After 30 days on the picket line, nurses at Montefiore, Mount Sinai Hospital, and Mount Sinai Morningside and West ratified new contracts with improved staffing ratios, workplace violence protections, and preserved healthcare benefits. Approximately 10,500 of the striking nurses returned to work.

The holdout was NewYork-Presbyterian, where nurses remained on strike. NYP had been the most aggressive of the three hospital systems — threatening job loss for striking nurses and refusing NYSNA's proposals on staffing ratios. The hospital was also hit with nearly $400,000 in financial remedies on February 17 after an arbitration finding of understaffing in a pediatric intensive care unit during the strike.
