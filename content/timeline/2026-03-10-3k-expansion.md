---
title: "1,000+ New 3-K Seats Across 56 ZIP Codes"
date: 2026-03-10
tags: [education]
---

Mamdani announced the addition of more than 1,000 new 3-K seats across 56 ZIP codes, pushing total citywide slots above 40,000. The expansion targets communities based on historical enrollment patterns, early application data, and provider capacity — five ZIP codes in the Bronx, six on Staten Island, eight in Brooklyn, sixteen in Manhattan, and twenty-one in Queens.

Staten Island was notably excluded from the initial 2-Care free childcare program announced in January. The administration said the borough was always part of the 2027 rollout, but some Staten Island residents objected to being left out of the first round. The 3-K expansion partially addresses that gap, though the island remains the only borough without 2-Care access in year one.

Mamdani cited the Quebec universal childcare study — for every dollar invested, thirteen dollars returned, and women's workforce participation increased sixteen percent. Chancellor Kamar Samuels framed it as meeting families where they are. This is the second childcare move in ten weeks, following the $1.7 billion state partnership with Hochul in January, and signals the administration's intent to make early childhood education its most visible domestic policy achievement.
