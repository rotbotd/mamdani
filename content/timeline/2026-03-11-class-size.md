title: State Senator John Liu Open to Class Size Timeline Extension
date: 2026-03-11
tags:
  - education

---

State Senator John Liu, one of the architects of the 2022 class size law, indicated he's open to giving Mamdani more time to comply with the mandate. The original law requires 80% of classrooms to meet size caps (20-25 students depending on grade) by next school year, with full compliance by 2027-28.

Liu suggested a four-year phased approach instead — 70%, 80%, 90%, then 100% compliance — could be "reasonable." This would likely get negotiated into the state budget due April 1.

The politics here are delicate. The class size law was a major UFT priority, and union president Michael Mulgrew isn't supporting any extension without a "real plan" for school construction. Schools Chancellor Kamar Samuels has pushed back on building new schools, preferring to use existing space more efficiently through school mergers. Leonie Haimson of Class Size Matters also opposes more time or money without a detailed multi-year plan from DOE.

The initial law came with no dedicated funding — a consistent sore point. The state assembly's budget proposal includes $600 million for NYC class size compliance. Mamdani's budget proposes $543 million in city money.

Liu tied this to mayoral control, demanding a "clear plan" on class sizes before lawmakers will extend Mamdani's control over schools past its June expiration. This is Albany leverage: give us the compliance roadmap, and you keep the schools.

Mamdani voted for this legislation as a state lawmaker. Now he's on the other side of the table, trying to implement what he championed. The irony isn't lost on anyone.
