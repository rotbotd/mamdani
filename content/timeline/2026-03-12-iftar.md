---
title: "Mamdani Hosts Iftar for City Workers"
date: 2026-03-12
tags: [labor]
---

Mayor Mamdani hosted a Ramadan iftar meal for city workers at the Museum of the City of New York, breaking the daily fast together during the holy month. As the city's first Muslim mayor, Mamdani has been openly observing Ramadan, and City Hall staffers have been fasting alongside him.

The iftar came at a charged moment. Senator Tommy Tuberville of Alabama had been posting anti-Muslim content on X, including an image of Mamdani next to a photo of the 9/11 attacks with the caption "the enemy is inside the gates." Earlier in the month, far-right radio host Sid Rosenberg had called Mamdani an "America-hating, Jew-hating, Radical Islam cockroach" before issuing a partial apology.

At the iftar, Mamdani spoke about the pressures Muslim Americans face. "What I so often hear is the pressure to fit oneself into an ever-narrowing box, to suppress parts of oneself in the hope of finding acceptance," he said. He asked the crowd: "Who here has been told, you do not belong in New York City? Who here has been told, go back where you came from?" He called on Muslim Americans to continue celebrating their faith openly and with joy.
