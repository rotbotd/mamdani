---
title: "Mamdani Hosts Iftar, Addresses Anti-Muslim Attacks"
date: 2026-03-12
tags: [immigration]
---

At an iftar dinner for Muslim city workers at the Museum of the City of New York, Mamdani directly addressed a week of escalating Islamophobic attacks from national Republican figures. Representative Brandon Gill of Texas called a photo of the mayor at an earlier iftar "stomach churning." Senator Tommy Tuberville of Alabama posted side-by-side images of the iftar and the World Trade Center on 9/11 with the caption "The enemy is inside the gates." Queens Councilwoman Vickie Paladino shared posts associating the mayor with Islamic terrorism.

Mamdani named each politician individually. "When I hear such hatred and disdain unchecked in its rancor, I feel an isolation and a loneliness that I know that many of you have felt as well," he told the room. He connected the rhetoric to real violence — a twelve-year-old girl in a hijab hospitalized in February after being punched in the face in Brooklyn, and the bombing attempt at Gracie Mansion five days earlier.

The speech echoed one from his campaign, when he held a press conference outside a Bronx mosque to condemn Islamophobic attacks from opponents. But this time he was speaking as mayor, during Ramadan, days after someone tried to bomb his home, to an audience of city employees who shared his faith and his vulnerability. Brooklyn College professor Moustafa Bayoumi noted the obvious strategy: "There's a very strong push to make Mamdani either lose his cool or not be able to govern New York City because of the distractions from the kind of attacks that he's going to be dealing with." Councilwoman Shahana Hanif said it gave Muslim leaders courage to speak out.

The first Muslim mayor of America's largest city, governing through Ramadan, under threat, being called a jihadist and a cockroach by talk radio hosts and compared to 9/11 by sitting senators. The historical weight of the moment was not subtle.
