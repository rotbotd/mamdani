---
title: "Largest Nurse Strike in NYC History Begins"
date: 2026-01-12
tags: [labor, healthcare]
---

Nearly 15,000 NYSNA nurses walked off the job at Mount Sinai Hospital, Mount Sinai Morningside and West, Montefiore, and NewYork-Presbyterian, launching the largest nurse strike in New York City history. The unfair labor practice strike came after months of stalled bargaining over safe staffing ratios, healthcare benefits for nurses, and workplace violence protections.

The three hospital systems are among the wealthiest in the city. As of September 2025, they held over $1.6 billion in cash and cash equivalents — twice their 2017 levels, even adjusted for inflation. They had already spent over $100 million on temporary replacement nurses rather than settle contracts. NewYork-Presbyterian CEO Steve Corwin made $26.3 million in total compensation in 2024, nearly 12,000 percent more than frontline registered nurses.

New York's eight safety-net hospitals and Northwell Health's three Long Island hospitals had already reached tentative agreements with NYSNA to avert strikes. The academic medical centers refused. Attorney General Letitia James, Public Advocate Jumaane Williams, four borough presidents, and the AFL-CIO all joined nurses on the picket line on day one.

Mamdani had positioned himself as labor's mayor. The strike would test whether that meant anything beyond rhetoric.
