## Erin Dalton Appointed DSS Commissioner

Mamdani appointed Erin Dalton as Commissioner of the Department of Social Services, which oversees both the Human Resources Administration (the nation's largest social services agency, serving 3 million New Yorkers) and the Department of Homeless Services.

Dalton comes from Allegheny County (Pittsburgh area), where she led one of the most innovative integrated human services systems in the country. Her track record is directly relevant to Mamdani's community safety vision:

She built a coordinated winter shelter network that reduced the number of people living outdoors by nearly 98%. She redesigned the county's mobile crisis response system, deploying behavioral health professionals to respond to human service 911 calls — essentially piloting the kind of civilian crisis response that the Department of Community Safety aims to scale citywide.

Under her leadership, violent crime declined by nearly 20% and incarceration rates fell by almost 10%. She also pioneered ethical, data-informed approaches to reduce racial disparities in child welfare decisions.

This is the "out of state" pick that gothamist reported on — bringing someone with a proven track record of the exact reforms Mamdani wants to implement.
