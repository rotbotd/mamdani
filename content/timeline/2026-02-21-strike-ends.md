---
title: "Historic 41-Day Nurse Strike Ends"
date: 2026-02-21
tags: [labor, healthcare]
---

After 41 days, the largest nurse strike in New York City history ended when nurses at NewYork-Presbyterian reached a tentative agreement. The contract included strengthened safe staffing ratios, workplace violence protections, and full preservation of healthcare benefits — the core demands NYSNA had been fighting for since bargaining began.

The strike's arc told a broader story about power in Mamdani's New York. The safety-net hospitals settled before the strike began. Montefiore and Mount Sinai settled after 30 days. NewYork-Presbyterian, the wealthiest and most intransigent, held out the longest — and ultimately conceded the most, including the $400,000 in financial penalties for understaffing during the strike.

Mamdani had visited the picket line multiple times, brought Sanders, and used the bully pulpit to pressure hospital management. Whether the mayor's involvement materially accelerated the outcome is debatable — NYSNA's strike fund and member solidarity were the decisive factors — but the political alignment was unmistakable. For the first time in decades, City Hall was unambiguously on labor's side during a major private-sector strike.
