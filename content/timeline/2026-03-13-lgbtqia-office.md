---
title: "Executive Order Establishes Mayor's Office of LGBTQIA+ Affairs"
date: 2026-03-13
tags: []
---

Mayor Mamdani signed an executive order establishing the Mayor's Office of LGBTQIA+ Affairs and appointed Taylor Brown as its director. Brown, an Assistant Attorney General in the Civil Rights Bureau of the New York State Attorney General's office, becomes the first transgender person to lead a New York City office or agency and the highest-ranking transgender person in the history of New York City government.

The office will oversee LGBTQIA+ initiatives across city agencies, develop legal resources to uphold sanctuary protections for LGBTQIA+ individuals, advance anti-discrimination measures, and provide support for LGBTQIA+ people and families fleeing anti-LGBTQIA+ oppression in other states. It absorbs and expands upon the NYC Unity Project, which launched in 2017 as the city's first coordinated initiative for LGBTQIA+ New Yorkers.

Brown's background is in impact litigation — nearly seven years at Lambda Legal and the ACLU's LGBTQ+ & HIV Project, leading constitutional cases on transgender healthcare access, education, and employment. Deputy Mayor for Economic Justice Julie Su framed the appointment through an economic justice lens, noting that housing insecurity, workplace discrimination, and healthcare access problems "are felt disproportionately" by LGBTQ+ New Yorkers.

The announcement came amid escalating federal attacks on LGBTQIA+ rights. State Senator Jabari Brisport, the first openly queer legislator of color elected to state office in New York, said the office "sends a message that our community matters."
