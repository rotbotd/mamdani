---
title: "Mamdani Attends Rental Ripoff Hearing in the Bronx"
date: 2026-03-11
tags: [housing]
---

Mayor Mamdani made his first in-person appearance at the city's "Rental Ripoff" hearings, held at Fordham University in the Bronx. The event was contentious. NYCHA residents heckled the mayor from the start, with one woman shouting "Public housing first mayor, public housing first" as he began speaking. The Crisis Action Center, which advocates for public housing residents, parked a mobile billboard outside mocking Mamdani with the message "Hi I'm Mayor Mamdani and I don't care about NYCHA!"

Mamdani addressed the criticism directly, acknowledging that "NYCHA residents faced chronic disinvestment" and that public housing tenants "have come to expect little from a city government that has constantly turned a blind eye." He said the administration understood its responsibility to "rewrite that legacy" and welcomed NYCHA residents' stories at the hearings.

The mayor announced that his administration would release a comprehensive housing plan in early May incorporating ideas surfaced during the hearings. He also highlighted the need to "build a more effective 311 response system" for housing complaints.

This was the third Rental Ripoff hearing. The first, in Brooklyn on February 26, was headlined by tenant advocate Cea Weaver and saw a protester storm the stage demanding NYCHA inclusion. At that hearing, roughly 500 renters who signed up for public testimony were instead offered one-on-one meetings with Weaver. The next hearing is scheduled for East Harlem on March 28.
