---
title: "Stanley Richards Appointed Correction Commissioner"
date: 2026-01-31
tags: [housing, criminal-justice]
---

Mamdani appointed Stanley Richards as Commissioner of the New York City Department of Correction — the first formerly incarcerated person to lead the agency. Richards had spent time on Rikers Island as a young man before going on to lead The Fortune Society, one of the city's most prominent reentry organizations.

The appointment was both symbolic and substantive. Richards inherited a department under federal court scrutiny, with a mandate from Mamdani to bring Rikers into compliance with Board of Correction minimum standards within 45 days and to implement Local Law 42 limiting solitary confinement beyond four hours. The broader goal — closing Rikers entirely and replacing it with four borough-based jails — remained the administration's stated policy, though no timeline had been set.

Criminal justice reform organizations praised the pick. Brooklyn Defenders called it a "meaningful step." The Fortune Society, which Richards built into a national model for reentry services, issued a statement supporting its former leader while urging the administration to move quickly on Rikers closure.
