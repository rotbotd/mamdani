---
title: "Historic $2.1M Court Judgment Against Bronx Landlord"
date: 2026-03-12
tags: [housing]
---

Mayor Mamdani and Corporation Counsel Steve Banks announced a first-of-its-kind ruling against the owners of 919 Prospect Avenue in the South Bronx. The ruling is the first to impose the maximum penalties available under New York City's Nuisance Abatement Law, requiring landlord Seth Miller — a consistent presence on the Public Advocate's "Worst Landlords Watchlist" — to address the most severe building violations within two weeks, all violations within one month, and pay $1,000 for each day a public nuisance persisted, totaling a retroactive penalty of $2,174,000.

The building's violations included a deteriorated facade, unsafe electrical equipment, obstructed fire escapes, an unsafe elevator, an inoperable boiler, a partial vacate order, peeling lead-based paint, lack of self-closing doors, and mice and roach infestations. Tenants represented by TakeRoot Justice had been fighting for livable conditions since 2016.

Mamdani called it "a landmark victory not only for those who call 919 Prospect Ave home, but for tenants across the five boroughs." Cea Weaver, director of the Mayor's Office to Protect Tenants, framed it as proof that "the Mayor of New York City is on tenants' side."

The judgment resulted from what the administration described as ramped-up, comprehensive enforcement against building owners who put tenants at risk, led by the Law Department in cooperation with the Mayor's Office to Protect Tenants and multiple city agencies.
