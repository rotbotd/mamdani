---
title: "Citywide Pothole Blitz Launched"
date: 2026-03-13
tags: [transit]
---

Mayor Mamdani and DOT Commissioner Mike Flynn announced a citywide pothole repair blitz following a historically brutal winter with record snowfall and sustained cold. More than 80 DOT crews would be deployed starting at 6 a.m. Saturday to fill thousands of potholes across all five boroughs in a single day, with additional blitzes planned in the weeks ahead.

Since January 1, DOT has repaired more than 50,000 potholes with a response time of just over two days. Over 10,000 potholes were filled in the past week alone. The blitz coincides with the start of the annual road resurfacing season, with DOT planning to resurface 1,150 lane miles of roadway this year. New York City is one of the few cities in the country that operates its own asphalt plants, allowing faster response times.
