## Bellevue Shelter Closing

The administration announced plans to close the 30th Street Shelter (Bellevue) by end of April 2026. The building, which opened in 1931, is in "severe state of disrepair" — a consequence of the Adams administration essentially stopping new shelter sitings toward the end of his term, leaving existing facilities to deteriorate.

About 250 people currently live there. DSS is working to relocate them by mid-March to other facilities. The city is maintaining the same total number of shelter beds.

New intake locations starting May 1:
- Adult men: 8 E 3rd St
- Adult families (no minor children): 333 Bowery St

DHS will keep a small presence at the old site for at least a year to redirect anyone who shows up there.

Mamdani committed to opening "several new, high-quality shelters" — this closure is framed as necessary infrastructure turnover rather than reduction of capacity. The long-term redevelopment plan for the Bellevue site is still being worked out.

## $50 Million for 10 Parks in Underserved Areas

Mamdani and Parks Commissioner Tricia Shimamura announced $50 million in capital investments through the Community Parks Initiative (CPI) for FY2027. The program, launched in 2014, targets parks that haven't seen significant investment in 20+ years in high-poverty, high-density neighborhoods.

The 10 parks getting reconstructed:

**Bronx:** Mott Playground (Concourse), Fountain of Youth Playground (Mott Haven/Longwood), Morris Mesa Playground (Mount Hope)

**Brooklyn:** Van Dyke Playground (Brownsville), Roebling Playground (South Williamsburg), Elizabeth Stroud Playground (Bed-Stuy)

**Manhattan:** Vladeck Park (Lower East Side), St. Nicholas Park 133rd St. Playground (Harlem)

**Queens:** Corona Health Sanctuary (Corona)

**Staten Island:** Kaltenmeier Playground (Rosebank/Shore Acres)

CUNY research cited alongside the announcement found CPI renovations led to more park time (especially among families and Latino residents), higher satisfaction with park quality, and reduced stress levels among frequent users.

This brings CPI to 57 active projects, with 70 already completed over the past decade — over half a billion dollars total. The investments are equity-driven: parks are selected based on poverty levels, population density, and growth.

Context: Mamdani promised 1% of the city budget for parks. His preliminary budget keeps parks funding flat at about half that. This $50 million announcement is capital investment (one-time construction money), not operating budget (ongoing maintenance and staffing). The final budget is due July 1.
