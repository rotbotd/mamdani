---
title: "Major 3-K Expansion Announced"
date: 2026-03-10
tags: [education]
---

Mayor Mamdani announced the addition of more than 1,000 new 3-K seats across 56 ZIP codes in all five boroughs, a major step toward making 3-K genuinely universal for the first time in the program's history.

The expansion targets neighborhoods identified through enrollment patterns, application data, and provider capacity. Families who have already applied do not need to take action; new programs will be added to MySchools and families notified as they are confirmed.

This announcement follows the administration's [2-K launch](/timeline/2026-03-03-childcare) earlier in the month and responds to years of cuts and unfulfilled universality promises under the Adams administration. The geographic spread is notable: Bronx gets 5 ZIP codes, Staten Island 6, Brooklyn 8, Manhattan 16, and Queens 21.

Council Member Jennifer Gutiérrez, chair of the Subcommittee on Early Childhood Education, noted the shift from the previous years: "For four years, I fought tooth and nail just to make sure we weren't losing seats, so to see this administration committing to 3-K expansion into every borough is nothing short of a breath of fresh air."

The administration is partnering with Governor Hochul and bringing in new providers for the first time in five years through a recent Request for Information process.
