---
title: Dueling Protests at Gracie Mansion, FBI Investigating Suspicious Devices
date: 2026-03-07
tags: [security, islamophobia]
---

A far-right anti-Muslim protest organized by January 6th figure Jake Lang brought about 20 people to Gracie Mansion, where they were met by roughly 120 counterprotesters. The situation escalated when a member of the anti-Muslim group allegedly pepper-sprayed a counterprotester and was arrested.

Then things got more serious. An 18-year-old counterprotester was seen lighting and throwing a device toward police. According to Commissioner Tisch, witnesses reported seeing flames and smoke as it traveled through the air before striking a barrier and extinguishing itself a few feet from officers. The suspect allegedly received another device from a 19-year-old, lit it, ran with it, and dropped it. Both were arrested.

The devices appeared to be jars wrapped in black tape containing nuts, bolts, and screws, with a hobby fuse. The FBI's Joint Terrorism Task Force is investigating whether they were functional improvised explosive devices or hoax devices. No injuries were reported.

Mayor Mamdani and his wife Rama Duwaji were confirmed safe. The mayor's spokesman called Lang a "vile white supremacist" and condemned the protest as "despicable and Islamophobic."

Six arrests total: the two suspects with devices, the pepper spray suspect, and three for disorderly conduct and blocking traffic.

This is the kind of thing that comes with being the first Muslim mayor of a major American city. The far right will keep testing, and the security situation around Mamdani will remain heightened. The counterprotester who threw the device did not help — giving ammunition to narratives about "both sides" violence, potentially facing serious federal charges, and creating a situation where the story becomes about them rather than about far-right intimidation of an elected official.
