---
title: "Hochul and Mamdani Announce $1.7 Billion Childcare Plan"
date: 2026-01-08
tags: [education]
---

One week into the administration, Governor Hochul and Mayor Mamdani stood together to announce a $1.7 billion investment toward universal childcare in New York — the largest single childcare expansion in the state's history. The plan would make childcare free for all two-year-olds in New York City, with the goal of eventually covering all children from six weeks to five years old.

The $1.7 billion represented new state funding on top of existing commitments, bringing total statewide investment to $4.5 billion for childcare and pre-kindergarten services. The bulk — $1.2 billion — would go toward expanding slots and subsidizing providers. The remainder covered pay increases for childcare workers, a step toward the pay parity with public school teachers that Mamdani had promised during the campaign.

Universal free childcare was one of Mamdani's two headline promises alongside the rent freeze. The Hochul partnership was significant — it signaled that Albany, at least on this issue, was willing to work with the socialist mayor rather than freeze him out. Whether that cooperation would extend to his tax proposals and housing agenda remained to be seen.
