---
title: "Sidewalk Shed Reforms Announced"
date: 2026-03-06
tags:
  - housing
  - quality-of-life
---

Mamdani announced reforms to reduce the number of sidewalk sheds blanketing NYC streets. NYCHA is spending $650 million to complete facade repairs and remove sheds at 40 developments, with over 200 buildings already completed.

The Department of Buildings is changing the rules in several ways: facade inspections will be required every 6 years instead of 5, well-maintained buildings can go 12 years between hands-on inspections, and sheds will only need to extend 40 feet from buildings instead of half the building's height. New penalties kick in for sheds left up longer than 180 days.

This is a quality-of-life issue that affects how people experience the city daily. Sidewalk sheds darken streets, create obstacles, and often stay up for years because landlords delay repairs. The 17% decline in sheds citywide shows the approach is working.

Mamdani framed it simply: "In the greatest city in the world, we should not accept darkened sidewalks and covered walkways as a fact of life."
