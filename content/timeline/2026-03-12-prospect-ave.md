---
title: "$2.1M Judgment Against Bronx Slumlord — First Maximum Penalty Under Nuisance Abatement Law"
date: 2026-03-12
tags: [housing]
---

The administration secured a first-of-its-kind court judgment against the owners of 919 Prospect Avenue in the South Bronx — the first case to impose the maximum penalties available under the city's Nuisance Abatement Law. The landlord, Seth Miller, a fixture on the Public Advocate's "Worst Landlords Watchlist," was ordered to fix the most severe violations within two weeks, all violations within one month, and pay $2,174,000 in retroactive penalties at $1,000 per day the nuisance persisted.

The building is rent-stabilized. The tenants had been fighting since 2016 through TakeRoot Justice for basic habitability — peeling lead paint, pest infestations, broken building systems, unsafe electrical equipment. The kind of conditions that define what tenant advocates mean when they talk about landlord neglect as a displacement strategy: let the building rot until people leave, then renovate and deregulate.

Corporation Counsel Steve Banks, Deputy Mayor for Housing Leila Bozorg, tenant protection director Cea Weaver, and HPD Commissioner Dina Levy all spoke. The message was coordinated and pointed: this administration would use every enforcement mechanism available to hold landlords accountable. Whether the $2.1 million is actually collectible, and whether the repairs actually happen within the court-ordered timeline, will be the real test.
