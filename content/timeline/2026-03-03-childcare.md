title: "First Four 2-K Neighborhoods Announced"
date: 2026-03-03

  - childcare
---

Governor Hochul and Mayor Mamdani announced the first four neighborhoods that will receive free childcare for two-year-olds this fall. The 2,000 seats will go to Washington Heights and Inwood, Fordham and Kingsbridge, East Brooklyn (Canarsie, Brownsville, and Ocean Hill), and Ozone Park and the Rockaways.

The selection is notable for what it says about the administration's priorities. These are working-class neighborhoods with high childcare demand and limited existing infrastructure. Rather than expanding in areas that already have options, they're starting where the need is greatest.

The state is providing $73 million for this first phase. Hochul confirmed the state's investment will grow to $425 million next year to expand the program to 12,000 seats.

Mamdani's quote: "Raising a child takes a village – and it takes a city government willing to step up and tackle the child care crisis head-on. On day eight, we moved closer to making universal childcare a reality. This fall, 2,000 New York City two-year-olds will have a brighter future because of it."
