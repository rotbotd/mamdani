title: "Mamdani Responds to One-House Budget Resolutions"
date: 2026-03-10T18:00:00
tags: [albany]
---

The State Assembly and Senate released their one-house budget resolutions, and Mayor Mamdani issued a statement welcoming several provisions while pushing for more.

Key points of alignment between the city and legislature:

The Assembly and Senate budgets recognize the scale of NYC's inherited fiscal crisis and include provisions to ask more from wealthy New Yorkers rather than cutting services. Both houses extended the fare-free bus pilot program, which Mamdani highlighted given that "one in five New Yorkers struggle to pay for public transportation."

Mamdani also emphasized the importance of mayoral control over schools, arguing it was essential to launching 2-K and fixing 3-K's longstanding problems. The extension of mayoral control remains a live negotiation as the budget process continues.

The statement reflects the broader Mamdani approach to Albany relations: express gratitude for partnership, claim credit for progressive provisions, and use the moment to push for outstanding priorities. The budget deadline is April 1, leaving three weeks for final negotiations.
