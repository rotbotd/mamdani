Two men from Pennsylvania — 18-year-old Emir Balat and 19-year-old Ibrahim Kayumi — threw homemade explosive devices at an anti-Islam protest organized outside Gracie Mansion by Jake Lang, a pardoned January 6 rioter and far-right influencer. One device was ignited but failed to detonate. The devices contained TATP, the same volatile explosive used in multiple European terror attacks, packed into glass jars with nuts, bolts, and screws as fragmentation. The FBI said the devices could have killed and maimed numerous people.

Balat allegedly told investigators he wanted an attack bigger than the Boston Marathon bombing. Both men pledged allegiance to ISIS — Balat in writing while in custody. A third device was found the next day in a vehicle three blocks from Gracie Mansion, along with handwritten notes listing TATP chemical ingredients.

The context was layered. The anti-Islam protest was targeting Mamdani specifically as the city's first Muslim mayor. Over a hundred counter-protesters had also gathered. The suspects, angered by the anti-Islam demonstration, brought the bombs intending to attack the protesters — meaning the violence was directed at the far-right demonstrators, not at the mayor, but at a protest defined by hostility toward his identity. The FBI's Joint Terrorism Task Force took the lead. Federal charges included attempted material support to a foreign terrorist organization, use of a weapon of mass destruction, and unlawful possession of destructive devices.

Law enforcement sources noted that ISIS, al-Qaeda, and pro-Iranian groups had intensified online recruiting since the U.S.-Iran war began roughly a week earlier, during Ramadan — a period when intelligence agencies had seen impressionable young men feel justified to act.

Mamdani's response was measured: "They should be held fully accountable for their actions. We will continue to keep New Yorkers safe. We will not tolerate terrorism or violence in our city." Commissioner Tisch emphasized the planned nature of the attack and the ISIS inspiration. The incident crystallized a reality the administration would face throughout its tenure — a Muslim socialist mayor governing through a period of intensifying anti-Muslim hostility, geopolitical crisis, and domestic extremism from multiple directions simultaneously.
