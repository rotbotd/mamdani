---
title: "Executive Order 13: Strengthening Sanctuary City Protections"
date: 2026-02-06
tags: [immigration]
---

At the city's annual Interfaith Breakfast, Mamdani signed Executive Order 13 — one of the strongest sanctuary city orders in the country. The order barred ICE from entering any city property, including schools, shelters, hospitals, parks, and parking garages, without a judicial warrant. It prohibited sharing city data with federal immigration authorities and required each city agency to appoint a privacy officer within 14 days and audit compliance with sanctuary laws.

The order also created an Interagency Response Committee to coordinate the city's response to federal immigration enforcement and launched a "Know Your Rights" campaign distributing over 32,000 multilingual flyers in ten languages through faith institutions across all five boroughs.

The timing was deliberate. The Trump administration had been escalating immigration enforcement nationally, and ICE operations in New York had intensified. Mamdani's order drew a line: city resources would not be used for federal deportation operations, and city property was off-limits without court authorization. The New York Legal Assistance Group, the New York Immigration Coalition, and dozens of faith leaders endorsed the order immediately.

This was Mamdani's thirteenth executive order in thirty-seven days — a pace that reflected both the urgency of the moment and the administration's preference for executive action while waiting for Albany on its larger legislative agenda.
