---
title: "Mamdani Completes McGuinness Boulevard Redesign on Day 3"
date: 2026-01-03
tags: [transit, streets]
---

On his third day in office, Mamdani announced the city would complete the full safety redesign of McGuinness Boulevard in Greenpoint, Brooklyn — a project that had become a symbol of the Adams administration's corruption and indifference to street safety.

The original DOT redesign would have reduced McGuinness from four lanes to two, adding protected bike lanes and wider sidewalks along a corridor where multiple pedestrians and cyclists had been killed. Adams killed the project in 2023 after intervention from a Brooklyn Democratic Party boss later indicted on corruption charges related to the decision. The watered-down version Adams approved removed the lane reduction entirely.

Mamdani's DOT commissioner Mike Flynn — appointed literally as the oath was being taken on January 1 — stood beside the mayor and confirmed the full original design would proceed. Flynn also announced the revival of four other bus and bike lane projects killed under Adams: offset bus lanes on Fordham Road serving 130,000 daily riders, a protected bike lane on Ashland Place in Brooklyn, and bike networks in East Flatbush and Ditmas Park.

Three days in and the administration was already undoing Adams's legacy project by project.
