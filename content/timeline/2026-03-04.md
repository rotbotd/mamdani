## DCWP Sues Predatory Tow Truck Operator

The administration's junk fees crackdown got its first tow truck target: Instant Recovery Corp., a Bronx-based operator that's been preying on New Yorkers since November 2024.

DCWP's investigation found the company systematically overcharged for towing, storage, and drop fees beyond legal limits, conducted illegal tows, forced cash-only payments, and refused to provide receipts. DCWP is seeking full consumer restitution, civil penalties for over a thousand violations, and license revocation.

This follows Executive Order 09 (the junk fees order from January). Recent enforcement actions include: banning hidden hotel "destination fees" and surprise credit card holds, suing solar panel installer Radiant Solar for undisclosed dealer fees, sweeping paid tax preparers for illegal overcharging, and "Fee Free February" targeting predatory employment agencies.

Mamdani: "Tow trucks often meet New Yorkers on their worst days — after an accident or a breakdown. Instead of offering help, companies like Instant Recovery have taken advantage of people when they need help most."

DCWP also conducted a compliance blitz last week, issuing warnings to 300+ tow operators citywide.
