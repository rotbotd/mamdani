---
title: "State Legislature One-House Budgets Back Mamdani's Tax Proposals"
date: 2026-03-11
tags: [albany]
---

The New York State Senate and Assembly released their one-house budget resolutions, and both chambers backed Mayor Mamdani's proposed tax increases on the wealthy. The proposals could raise roughly $5 billion in additional revenue, a significant win for the administration which has been pushing Albany to help address the city's fiscal crisis.

Mamdani responded: "I'm grateful that the Assembly and Senate One-House Budgets recognize the scale of the fiscal crisis facing New York City." The City Council had separately identified nearly $1.7 billion in potential savings in Mamdani's preliminary budget.

The NYC Comptroller also released comments on the preliminary budget for Fiscal Year 2027, noting concerns about the financial plan for fiscal years 2026-2030.

Republicans slammed the legislature for siding with Mamdani. Governor Hochul has remained steadfast in refusing to raise taxes, setting up a fight in the final budget negotiations. The one-house resolutions are opening positions — the actual budget requires agreement between the Senate, Assembly, and Governor.
