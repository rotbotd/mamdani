---
title: "Mamdani Condemns Tuberville's Anti-Muslim Posts"
date: 2026-03-13
tags: []
---

Mayor Mamdani publicly condemned a series of anti-Muslim social media posts by Republican Senator Tommy Tuberville of Alabama. On X, Tuberville had reposted an image of Mamdani next to a photo of the 9/11 attacks with the words "the enemy is inside the gates." He also falsely claimed that "Americans are being gunned down in the streets almost daily by Radical Islamists," a claim contradicted by a 2025 Center for Strategic and International Studies study finding that jihadist attacks in the U.S. are rare and "not resurgent."

Senate Democratic Minority Leader Chuck Schumer called Tuberville's posts "mindless hate," adding that "Muslim Americans are cops, doctors, nurses, teachers, bankers, bricklayers, mothers, fathers, neighbors, mayors, and more. Islamophobic hate like this is fundamentally un-American." Senator Bernie Sanders called it "nothing less than blatant Islamophobic racism." Republican leaders were largely silent.

The posts are part of a pattern of anti-Muslim hostility directed at Mamdani since taking office, intensifying during Ramadan. The Gracie Mansion bombing earlier in March, now under FBI terrorism investigation, has added a security dimension to the threats. Mamdani has consistently responded by centering Muslim Americans' experiences and calling for open celebration of faith rather than retreat.
