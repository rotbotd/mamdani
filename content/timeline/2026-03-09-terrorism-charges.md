title: Gracie Mansion Devices Now Investigated as ISIS-Related Terrorism
date: 2026-03-09

The explosive devices thrown at the March 7 protests outside Gracie Mansion are now being investigated as "ISIS-related terrorism," according to NYPD Commissioner Jessica Tisch. The two suspects, ages 18 and 19, are facing federal terrorism charges.

At a press conference at Gracie Mansion, Commissioner Tisch said the devices were functional improvised explosive devices containing shrapnel. The investigation is being handled by the FBI's Joint Terrorism Task Force.

The ISIS connection is strange and requires explanation. The suspects were counterprotesters — they were there to oppose the far-right anti-Muslim demonstration led by Jake Lang. The ideology doesn't fit the typical ISIS-inspired attack profile. It's possible the suspects had ISIS sympathies separate from their presence at the protest, or that federal investigators are being aggressive with terrorism designations to maximize charges. Details remain unclear.

Mayor Mamdani held his own remarks at the press conference. Notably, he continued to defend the right to peaceful protest even as he condemned the violence. "We will not let anyone — not white supremacists, not terrorists — intimidate New Yorkers from exercising their constitutional rights," he said. He was also heckled by Jake Lang, who shouted from the sidewalk during the presser.

The political dynamics are ugly. The far right will use this to paint counterprotesters as terrorists. Some will use the ISIS designation to attack Mamdani by association, despite him being the target of both the far-right protest and now this investigation. Mamdani's response — firmly condemning the violence while protecting protest rights — is the correct one, but it won't satisfy people looking to use this against him.
