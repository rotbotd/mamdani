title: Gracie Mansion Devices Now Investigated as ISIS-Related Terrorism
date: 2026-03-09
tags: [security, islamophobia]
---

The explosive devices thrown at the March 7 protests outside Gracie Mansion are now being investigated as "ISIS-inspired terrorism," according to NYPD Commissioner Jessica Tisch. The two suspects — Emir Balat, 18, and Ibrahim Kayumi, 19, both from Pennsylvania — are facing federal terrorism charges including "providing material support to a designated foreign terrorist organization" and "use of a weapon of mass destruction."

At a press conference at Gracie Mansion, Commissioner Tisch said at least one device contained TATP (triacetone triperoxide), a chemical widely used by bomb-makers globally. A third device, found Sunday in a nearby vehicle, did not contain explosive material. "These were not hoax devices nor smoke bombs. They were improvised explosive devices that could have caused serious injury or death," Tisch said.

According to court documents, Balat allegedly wrote that he "pledged allegiance to the Islamic state" and told police he wanted an attack "bigger than the Boston Marathon bombing." Kayumi allegedly told police he watched ISIS material and considered himself "affiliated" with ISIS. NYPD counter-terrorism chief Rebecca Weiner noted a pattern of "younger and younger individuals who are radicalizing and mobilizing to violence" through social media.

The ISIS connection complicates the narrative. The suspects were counterprotesters — they were there to oppose the far-right anti-Muslim demonstration led by Jake Lang. Yet they apparently held ISIS sympathies separate from their presence at the protest. Commissioner Tisch noted the city has been on "heightened alert since the start of hostilities in Iran." On February 28, the US and Israel launched major strikes against Iranian nuclear facilities and military targets — the largest military escalation in the Middle East since the June 2025 conflict. Iran has retaliated across the region. The federal government is treating this as active warfare, with Trump administration officials using eliminationist rhetoric ("annihilation"). This geopolitical context — an active US war against a Muslim-majority country — provides the backdrop for both the far-right protests and the radicalization of the suspects.

Mayor Mamdani held his own remarks at the press conference. Notably, he continued to defend the right to peaceful protest even as he condemned the violence. "We will not let anyone — not white supremacists, not terrorists — intimidate New Yorkers from exercising their constitutional rights," he said. He was also heckled by Jake Lang, who shouted from the sidewalk during the presser.

The political dynamics are ugly. The far right will use this to paint counterprotesters as terrorists. Some will use the ISIS designation to attack Mamdani by association, despite him being the target of both the far-right protest and now this investigation. Mamdani's response — firmly condemning the violence while protecting protest rights — is the correct one, but it won't satisfy people looking to use this against him.
