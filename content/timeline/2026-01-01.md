---
title: "Mamdani Inaugurated as 112th Mayor of New York City"
date: 2026-01-01
tags: [housing, labor]
---

Zohran Kwame Mamdani was sworn in as the 112th Mayor of New York City just after midnight at City Hall — the first DSA socialist and first Muslim mayor in the city's history. In his inaugural address, he pledged to govern "audaciously" and declared that "the era of austerity is over."

Within hours, he signed his first two executive orders. The first revoked all executive orders issued after September 26, 2024 — the day former Mayor Eric Adams was indicted — effectively wiping out Adams's late-term orders including those adopting the IHRA antisemitism definition and restricting Israel boycotts. The second revived the Mayor's Office to Protect Tenants, appointing prominent housing organizer Cea Weaver as director, and launched the LIFT Task Force to identify city-owned land for affordable housing and the SPEED Task Force to cut development red tape.

His first appointment had come even before inauguration: transit advocate Mike Flynn as DOT commissioner, announced the moment he took the oath. By January 3, Flynn had completed the full safety redesign of McGuinness Boulevard in Greenpoint and revived four bus and bike lane projects killed under Adams.

The signal was unmistakable — housing, transit, and a clean break from Adams.
